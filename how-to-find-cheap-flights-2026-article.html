The 3 sale patterns airlines follow Why fares drop at predictable times

Pattern 1: Tuesday morning sales

Airlines release sales at 12:00pm UTC Tuesday (often 7am or 8am Eastern). One airline starts, competitors match within 24 hours. This is the single-most predictable moment to catch deals. Set a calendar alert for Tuesday 7am your time.

Pattern 2: Oversupplied routes

Routes with three or more competing airlines get cheaper. New York ↔ Miami? Expensive (only major carriers fly it). But Newark ↔ Fort Lauderdale? Budget carriers compete hard. Use Skyscanner's "Everywhere" tool to find cheaper secondary airports.

Pattern 3: Mistake fares and price correction windows

Airlines occasionally misprice. Fares get corrected within 24–48 hours, but alert communities catch them instantly. Follow Secret Flying on Twitter and set Hopper to notify you of extreme drops.

💡 The insight:

You're not searching for random luck. You're hunting specific moments when fares are mathematically cheaper.

Timing: When to search, when to book Days, weeks, months that save the most

Best days to search

  • Tuesday morning: Catch the airline sales wave as it spreads.
  • Wednesday afternoon: Check for airline response sales and competitor matches.
  • Friday evening–Sunday: Last-minute deals from airlines dumping empty seats.

Best days to fly

  • Tuesday–Thursday: Save 30–50% vs. Friday–Sunday.
  • Early morning or red-eye: Cheaper than midday and evening.
  • Avoid holidays: Thanksgiving, Christmas, spring break = premium pricing.

Best booking window

  • Domestic: Book 3–6 weeks before departure.
  • International: Book 2–3 months before departure.
  • Exceptions: Set price alerts and watch for flash sales (book within 24–48 hours).
⚡ Pro move:

Book the Tuesday sale for a flight 6 weeks away, then fly Tuesday–Thursday. That's the double-discount sweet spot.
