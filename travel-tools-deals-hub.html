    Booking platforms & loyalty programs Where to actually book + how to earn

    Where to book (the boring but important part)

    Once you've found a good fare, book directly on the airline website or through a trusted aggregator like
    Google Flights, Skyscanner, or Kayak. Benefits:

    • Direct airline changes/cancellations (sometimes more flexible)
    • Earn frequent flyer miles directly with the airline
    • No middleman issues if something goes wrong

    Loyalty program strategy

    • Focus on 1-2 airlines: Better tier status faster than spreading miles across 5 carriers.
    • Co-branded credit cards: Earn miles faster, get perks (free checked bags, seat upgrades).
    • Transfer partners: Burn miles on premium cabins via partner airlines when value is strong.
    💰 Math check:

    If you fly 2-3 times annually, a co-branded card pays for itself in perks within one trip. Then it's pure upside.

    Setup your alerts (30 minutes, done once) Step-by-step to automate your deal hunting

    Your 30-minute setup

    1. Google Flights: Add your home airport + 5 favorite destinations. Enable price alerts.
    2. Hopper app: Download, sign in, add 3-5 routes you'd actually fly.
    3. Scott's: Subscribe to dail email from your home airport.
    4. Secret Flying: Follow on Twitter, enable notifications (set to important only).
    5. Calendar reminder: Every Tuesday, spend 15 min browsing the "Airlines Released Sales" Tuesday secret.
    🎯 Result:

    You'll catch 90% of deals before they disappear. Work 30 minutes, save thousands per year.
