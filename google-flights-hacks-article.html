The calendar view (the #1 hidden feature) See prices for every day at a glance

How to access it

  1. Go to Google Flights
  2. Enter departure city, arrival city
  3. Click the calendar icon instead of picking specific dates
  4. You'll see a grid of 30 days with prices for each day

Why this changes everything

You instantly see which days are cheap and which are expensive. The cheapest day might be $200 less than your preferred departure. This single feature has saved travelers thousands by simply showing them when to fly instead of when they want to fly.

Pro move:

Look for the greenest (cheapest) days. Plan your trip around those dates, not your calendar.

Flexible destination search Find the cheapest city, not just your planned one

How to use it

  1. In destination, enter a region or click "Flexible Destinations"
  2. Google shows fares to all airports in that region
  3. Often the secondary airport is 30-50% cheaper than the major hub

Real example

  • New York to London Heathrow: $800
  • New York to London Gatwick: $580 (same trip, different airport)
  • New York to London Stansted: $520 (further out, even cheaper)
💰 The math:

Save $300 on flights + $30 on ground transport to secondary airport = net savings $270+. Plus shorter connections and less crowded airports.

Setting price alerts (and actually using them) Get notified when prices drop 50%

The right way to set alerts

  1. Search your route on Google Flights
  2. Click the bell icon (price alert)
  3. Sign in to your Google account
  4. You'll receive email notifications when prices drop significantly

The pro setup

  • Set alerts 3–6 months before your planned trip (no closer)
  • Set for the cheap days from the calendar view, not your preferred dates
  • Create a separate email folder for Google Flights alerts so they don't clutter your inbox
⚡ Reality check:

If you get an alert and prices dropped $300, book immediately. That's a flash sale, not a trend. These disappear within 24 hours.

Departure time filtering Red-eyes cost less. Night flights save money.

The price by departure time

  • Midday (10am–4pm): Most expensive (business travelers)
  • Morning (5am–9am): Mid-range pricing
  • Evening (4pm–9pm): Mid-range but less convenient
  • Red-eye (9pm–5am): Cheapest by 30-50% (fewer people want to fly at night)

How to filter on Google Flights

  1. After searching, find the departure/arrival time sliders on the left
  2. Drag to select "Departs between 9pm–6am"
  3. Prices instantly drop
Pro philosophy:

Sacrifice one night on a red-eye to save $200–$400. Sleep on the plane, arrive fresh, save big.

Trip planner (combine outbound + return) Book the best combination, not just best individual flights

Google Flights automatically pairs your outbound and return flights. But you can manually swap them.

Example:
Outbound Tuesday 6am = $280
Return Friday 9pm = $320
Total = $600

But what if:
Outbound Tuesday 11pm (red-eye) = $140
Return Friday 9pm = $320
Total = $460 (save $140)

The key:

Don't assume the "Best" option is the best for your budget. Look at the list and combine the cheapest options manually.

Stops and layover length customization Long layovers sometimes cost less. Use them strategically.

The reality of layovers and pricing

  • Direct flights: Most expensive
  • 1 stop, short layover (2–4 hours): Mid-range
  • 1 stop, long layover (6–12 hours): Often 20-40% cheaper
  • 2+ stops: Cheapest but rarely worth it unless saving $200+

The pro move

If you have a 10-hour layover in a major city with free WiFi and a good lounge, book it. Use the time to work, walk around, or sleep. You save money and get a bonus mini-adventure.

Set the filter on Google Flights:

Click "Number of stops" and select "1 stop". Then look at layover lengths in the results. The longest layovers are cheapest.
