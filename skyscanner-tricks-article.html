Everywhere search (change destination to "Anywhere") Find the cheapest city from your location instantly

How it works

  1. Go to Skyscanner.net
  2. Enter your departure city
  3. In "Destination," type "Anywhere" or select all regions
  4. Pick your dates
  5. Search

What you'll see

A list of 50+ destinations sorted by price. The cheapest is first. You get to see all affordable options, not just your planned destination.

Real advantage over Google Flights

Google Flights shows a map of prices. Skyscanner shows you the actual list with airline and specific fares. You can sort by route, duration, and more.

💡 Pro move:

If flexible, book the cheapest destination shown, not your original plan. Save $300-$600 and discover somewhere new.

Route map visualization See all flights from your city in one visual

The feature you didn't know existed

After searching, click "Route Map" or find it in the left sidebar on Skyscanner. You'll see a visualization of flights from your city. Airlines appear as lines. Hover to see prices.

Why this matters

  • See connections you wouldn't normally think of (New York → Barcelona via Dublin)
  • Instantly spot which airlines service your route
  • Identify cheap connections by visual inspection
Travel hack:

Look at the route map and find routes with multiple connections. Often cheaper than direct alternatives.

Multi-city and round-trip combinations Book a loop for less than round-trip pricing

The strategy

Use Skyscanner's "Multi-city" option to build custom routes. Example:

  • Leg 1: New York → London (Tuesday, $400)
  • Leg 2: London → Paris (Friday, $120)
  • Leg 3: Paris → New York (Sunday, $380)
  • Total: $900

vs. a simple New York → London round-trip: $600 + London → Paris round-trip: $400 = $1,000 separately.

The advantage:

Multi-city gives you flexibility and often finds cheaper overall routings because airlines bundle differently.

Flexible dates + flexible destinations The most powerful combination on Skyscanner

Set both filters

  1. Click "Flexible dates" → select a month span, not specific dates
  2. Click "Flexible destinations" → select all regions
  3. Search

What you get back

A comprehensive list of all destinations, sorted by cheapest price. You can then filter by date, airline, or duration.

⚡ This is THE most powerful search on Skyscanner.

If you're flexible on everything, this finds deals no other site surface.

Incognito mode and price tracking Clear cookies to see real prices (Skyscanner's secret)

The trick nobody talks about

If you search the same route repeatedly, prices might appear higher because Skyscanner and airlines track your searches. They know you're serious and may show inflated prices.

The fix

  • Use incognito/private mode when searching (Chrome, Firefox, Safari all have this)
  • Clear cookies between searches
  • Or just use a different browser

Why this works

Airlines use cookies to track if you've viewed a flight before. They slightly increase dynamic pricing for repeat lookers. Fresh searches often show lower "base" prices.

Pro move:

Use Skyscanner in incognito mode the first time, note the price, then search in regular mode. See if prices jump.
